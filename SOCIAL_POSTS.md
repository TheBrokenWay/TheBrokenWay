## Post 1 — core thesis

Short version:

Most teams are trying to improve the model.

I’m building the governance layer that tells the model what it is allowed to
do.

That work started in a high-consequence science setting and eventually became
its own product family:

- PredX for governed scientific decision support
- CageOS for bounded operational governance
- CageOS Lite as the public proof surface

Public demo:
https://github.com/TheBrokenWay/CageOS-Lite-Demo

## Post 2 — CageOS Lite demo launch

I published a public proof surface for a simple claim:

capable models should not be trusted as their own governors.

CageOS Lite Demo shows a different pattern:

- visible agent surface
- independent governance layer
- bounded outcomes: allow, redirect, escalate, block

It is intentionally limited. The goal is to prove the governance pattern
without exposing the private control plane.

Repo:
https://github.com/TheBrokenWay/CageOS-Lite-Demo

## Post 3 — origin story

This work did not start as a governance project.

It started with a scientific and pharmaceutical decision-support problem.

The deeper I got, the clearer it became that better answers were not enough.
Prompting was not enough. Review after the fact was not enough.

The real problem was governance in the operating path.

That is how the work split into:

- PredX
- CageOS
- CageOS Lite

Public materials:
https://github.com/TheBrokenWay/TheBrokenWay

## Post 4 — direct enterprise angle

High-trust environments cannot rely on black-box decisions dressed up as
governance.

If a system is going to shape serious workflows, control has to stay outside
the model and inside an independent operating path.

That is the core idea behind CageOS.

Public proof:
https://github.com/TheBrokenWay/CageOS-Lite-Demo

## Post 5 — founder credibility post

I built this without a formal technical background, no big team, and no large
budget.

That does not make the problem smaller.

It means the standard has to be higher:

- public proof where possible
- private control paths where necessary
- no pretending prompt-only safety is the same thing as governance

That is the direction behind Tillar Technologies.
